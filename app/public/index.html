Welcome to your Personalized Movie Recommendation System

This is a general explanation for this Personalized Movie Recommendation System:

TMDB API serves all movie recommendations in this system. They seem to base their recommendations mostly on the behavior of their community such as similar rating and watching behaviors, among others. TMDB also defines which genres belong to an specific movie.

We are using their service to filter out these recommendations by movie GENRES as following:

  1. Based on 2 movies: "Parasite"(2020) and "The Shape Of Water"(2018), as your hypothetical personalized movie preference profile, we show a list of recommendations according to TMDB API recommendations
  2. Additionally, you can control these recommendations by choosing different levels of five movie emotions. Each movie emotion is mapped with a set of corresponding movie genres: (1)~Joy is mapped with genres as: Comedy, Animation, Family, and Musical; (2)~Scariness with: Horror, Mystery, and Thriller; (3)Loving: Romance, and Documentary; (4)~Grief: Drama; and (5)~Amusement: Adventure, Action, Crime, Science Fiction, War, and Western. The amount of preference that you choose for each emotion will determine the amount of movie recommendations that you will get in your recommendations. For example, the more Joy you prefer in comparison with other genres, the more comedies, animations, family, and musical movies will appear for the list of recommendations
  3. If you prefer random recommendations based on these 2 movies, "shake" the interface to reduce all emotion preferences to 0(zero). In this case, the recommendations are going to be presented randomly without considering any emotion or movie genres.
  4. Feel free to check the "?" mark in every movie, there is a specific explanation to know why it has been recommended At the bottom of every movie, you will also find a TMDB rating for that movie according to their community's voting. All shown movies will be ordered based on this rating from left to right, showing first the highest movies.
